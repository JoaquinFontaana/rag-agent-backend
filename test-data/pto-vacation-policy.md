# Paid Time Off (PTO) and Vacation Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department  
**Version:** 3.2

## 1. Overview

This policy outlines the company's approach to paid time off, including vacation days, sick leave, personal days, and holidays. We believe in work-life balance and encourage employees to take time off to rest and recharge.

## 2. Vacation Time Accrual

### 2.1 Accrual Rates
Vacation time accrues based on tenure:

| Years of Service | Annual Accrual | Per Pay Period (Bi-weekly) |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| 0-2 years       | 15 days        | 4.62 hours                |
| 3-5 years       | 20 days        | 6.15 hours                |
| 6-10 years      | 25 days        | 7.69 hours                |
| 10+ years       | 30 days        | 9.23 hours                |

### 2.2 Accrual Rules
- Vacation begins accruing from the first day of employment
- Accrual occurs on each pay date
- Part-time employees accrue on a pro-rated basis
- No accrual during unpaid leave periods

### 2.3 Maximum Accrual
- Employees may carry over a maximum of 1.5x their annual accrual
- Once the cap is reached, no additional time accrues until the balance decreases
- Employees will receive a notification when approaching the cap

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Accrual
- All employees accrue 1 day of sick leave per month (12 days annually)
- Sick leave accrues separately from vacation time
- Maximum carryover: 60 days

### 3.2 Eligible Uses
Sick leave may be used for:
- Personal illness or injury
- Medical, dental, or optical appointments
- Care for immediate family members who are ill
- Mental health days
- Preventive care

### 3.3 Documentation
- No documentation required for absences of 1-2 days
- Doctor's note required for 3+ consecutive days
- FMLA documentation may be required for extended absences

## 4. Personal Days

### 4.1 Allocation
All full-time employees receive 3 personal days per year:
- Granted on January 1st each year
- New hires receive pro-rated days based on start date
- Do not accrue or carry over

### 4.2 Usage
Personal days may be used for:
- Religious observances
- Family obligations
- Personal business that cannot be scheduled outside work hours
- Any reason without explanation required

## 5. Company Holidays

### 5.1 Observed Holidays
The company observes the following paid holidays:

1. New Year's Day (January 1)
2. Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Monday in January)
3. Presidents' Day (3rd Monday in February)
4. Memorial Day (Last Monday in May)
5. Independence Day (July 4)
6. Labor Day (1st Monday in September)
7. Thanksgiving Day (4th Thursday in November)
8. Day after Thanksgiving
9. Christmas Eve (December 24)
10. Christmas Day (December 25)
11. Two floating holidays (employee's choice)

### 5.2 Holiday Pay
- If a holiday falls on a weekend, it is observed on the nearest weekday
- Employees required to work on holidays receive holiday pay plus regular pay
- Part-time employees receive pro-rated holiday pay

## 6. Requesting Time Off

### 6.1 Request Process
1. Submit requests through the HR management system
2. Requests are subject to manager approval
3. Requests should be submitted as early as possible

### 6.2 Advance Notice Requirements
- 1-2 days: 1 week notice
- 3-5 days: 2 weeks notice
- 6+ days: 1 month notice
- Exceptions may be made for emergencies

### 6.3 Approval Criteria
Managers will consider:
- Business needs and staffing levels
- First-come, first-served basis for popular periods
- Employee's performance and attendance record
- Impact on team and projects

### 6.4 Blackout Periods
Certain departments may have blackout periods during peak business times:
- Finance: Month-end and quarter-end closing
- Retail: Holiday shopping season
- Customer Support: Product launch periods

## 7. Extended Leave

### 7.1 Sabbatical
Employees with 7+ years of service may request a sabbatical:
- Up to 4 weeks of unpaid leave
- Must be approved by department head
- Requires 6 months advance notice
- Limited to once every 5 years

### 7.2 Unpaid Leave
- Employees may request unpaid leave after exhausting PTO
- Approval is at company's discretion
- Benefits may be affected during extended unpaid leave

## 8. PTO Payout

### 8.1 Upon Termination
- Unused vacation time is paid out at the employee's current rate
- Sick leave is not paid out
- Personal days are not paid out
- Notice period must be served to receive payout

### 8.2 State Requirements
In states requiring PTO payout, company will comply with local laws.

## 9. Special Circumstances

### 9.1 Bereavement Leave
- Up to 5 days of paid leave for immediate family members
- Up to 3 days for extended family members
- Additional unpaid leave may be granted
- Immediate family: spouse, partner, child, parent, sibling
- Extended family: grandparent, grandchild, aunt, uncle, in-laws

### 9.2 Jury Duty
- Employees are granted paid leave for jury duty
- Must provide documentation from the court
- Any jury pay received is retained by the employee

### 9.3 Military Leave
- Company complies with USERRA requirements
- Military leave is unpaid but benefits continue
- Job restoration guaranteed upon return

### 9.4 Parental Leave
- See separate Parental Leave Policy
- Maternity, paternity, and adoption leave covered separately

## 10. Time Off Tracking

### 10.1 Employee Responsibilities
- Log all time off in the HR system
- Set out-of-office messages
- Notify team members of absences
- Ensure work coverage is arranged

### 10.2 Manager Responsibilities
- Review and respond to requests within 48 hours
- Ensure fair distribution of holiday time
- Monitor team coverage
- Track time-off balances

## 11. Policy Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Denial of future time-off requests
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination

Examples of violations:
- Taking unapproved time off
- Misrepresenting reason for absence
- Abuse of sick leave
- Excessive absenteeism

## 12. Questions

For questions about PTO balances or this policy:
- Check your balance in the HR portal
- Contact HR at hr@company.com
- Speak with your direct manager

This policy is subject to change and will be reviewed annually.
