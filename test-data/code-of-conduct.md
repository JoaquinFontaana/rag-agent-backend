# Employee Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources & Legal  
**Version:** 4.0

## 1. Introduction

This Code of Conduct outlines the principles and expectations that guide our behavior as employees of the company. Every employee is expected to read, understand, and comply with this code.

## 2. Core Values

Our company is built on these core values:

- **Integrity:** We act honestly and ethically in all situations
- **Respect:** We treat everyone with dignity and respect
- **Excellence:** We strive for quality in everything we do
- **Innovation:** We embrace creativity and continuous improvement
- **Collaboration:** We work together to achieve common goals
- **Accountability:** We take responsibility for our actions

## 3. Professional Conduct

### 3.1 General Standards
Employees are expected to:
- Arrive on time and maintain regular attendance
- Complete work assignments accurately and on time
- Follow company policies and procedures
- Represent the company positively
- Maintain professional appearance and demeanor
- Use company time and resources appropriately

### 3.2 Communication
- Communicate respectfully and professionally
- Listen actively and considerately
- Provide constructive feedback
- Avoid gossip and negative talk about colleagues
- Use appropriate language in all settings

### 3.3 Dress Code
- Business casual attire Monday-Thursday
- Casual Friday (jeans and appropriate tops)
- Client-facing employees should dress professionally
- Remote employees should dress appropriately for video calls
- Safety equipment required in designated areas

## 4. Respectful Workplace

### 4.1 Harassment Prevention
The company has zero tolerance for harassment, including:
- Sexual harassment
- Verbal or physical abuse
- Intimidation or bullying
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Creating a hostile work environment

### 4.2 Diversity and Inclusion
We are committed to:
- Equal employment opportunities
- Celebrating diversity
- Creating an inclusive environment
- Preventing discrimination
- Fostering belonging

### 4.3 Protected Characteristics
We do not discriminate based on:
- Race, color, national origin
- Religion or creed
- Sex, gender identity, or sexual orientation
- Age
- Disability
- Veteran status
- Genetic information
- Any other protected status

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

### 5.1 Definition
A conflict of interest occurs when personal interests interfere with company interests.

### 5.2 Examples
Potential conflicts include:
- Outside employment with competitors
- Financial interest in competing businesses
- Hiring family members
- Business dealings with family or friends
- Accepting gifts from vendors or clients
- Using company resources for personal gain

### 5.3 Disclosure
Employees must:
- Disclose any potential conflicts to their manager and HR
- Complete annual conflict of interest disclosure
- Recuse themselves from related decisions
- Seek guidance when uncertain

## 6. Confidentiality and Data Protection

### 6.1 Confidential Information
Employees must protect:
- Trade secrets and proprietary information
- Customer data and personal information
- Financial information
- Strategic plans and business strategies
- Employee personal information
- Unpublicized company information

### 6.2 Data Security
- Use strong passwords and change them regularly
- Lock computers when away from desk
- Do not share login credentials
- Report data breaches immediately
- Follow clean desk policy
- Dispose of sensitive documents securely

### 6.3 Social Media
When using social media:
- Do not disclose confidential information
- Do not speak on behalf of the company unless authorized
- Make it clear personal views are your own
- Be professional and respectful
- Comply with social media policy

## 7. Company Resources

### 7.1 Acceptable Use
Company resources (computers, phones, internet, supplies) should be:
- Used primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use is acceptable
- Not used for illegal activities
- Not used to access inappropriate content
- Protected from damage or theft

### 7.2 Technology Use
- Follow IT security policies
- Do not install unauthorized software
- Report technical issues promptly
- Do not attempt to bypass security measures
- Use company-approved collaboration tools

### 7.3 Intellectual Property
- All work created during employment belongs to the company
- Respect copyrights and trademarks
- Do not use unlicensed software
- Follow licensing agreements

## 8. Health and Safety

### 8.1 Safe Workplace
Employees must:
- Follow all safety procedures
- Report hazards immediately
- Use protective equipment when required
- Keep work areas clean and organized
- Participate in safety training

### 8.2 Substance Abuse
- The workplace must be free from drugs and alcohol
- No illegal drugs at any time
- No alcohol except at approved company events
- Prescription medications must not impair performance
- Report suspected substance abuse to HR

### 8.3 Violence Prevention
- Zero tolerance for workplace violence
- No weapons on company premises
- Report threats or concerning behavior
- Respect restraining orders
- Follow emergency procedures

## 9. Financial Integrity

### 9.1 Accurate Records
- Maintain accurate and complete records
- Submit truthful expense reports
- Record time worked honestly
- Do not falsify documents
- Cooperate with audits

### 9.2 Expense Reporting
- Submit expenses promptly with receipts
- Only claim legitimate business expenses
- Follow travel and expense policy
- Obtain required approvals
- Do not abuse company credit cards

### 9.3 Anti-Bribery and Corruption
- Do not offer or accept bribes
- Follow the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- Do not make improper payments to government officials
- Report suspected corruption
- Complete anti-corruption training

## 10. Regulatory Compliance

### 10.1 Legal Compliance
Employees must:
- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Understand laws relevant to their role
- Seek guidance when unsure
- Report potential violations
- Cooperate with investigations

### 10.2 Industry-Specific Regulations
Depending on your role, comply with:
- Data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA)
- Financial regulations (SOX, SEC rules)
- Export control regulations
- Environmental regulations
- Industry-specific certifications

## 11. Reporting Violations

### 11.1 Duty to Report
Employees must report:
- Violations of this code
- Illegal or unethical behavior
- Safety hazards
- Discrimination or harassment
- Conflicts of interest
- Fraud or financial irregularities

### 11.2 Reporting Channels
Report through:
- Direct manager
- HR Department (hr@company.com)
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX
- Anonymous web portal: ethics.company.com
- Legal Department for legal issues

### 11.3 Non-Retaliation
- The company prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports concerns in good faith
- Retaliation includes termination, demotion, harassment, or other adverse action
- Report retaliation immediately
- Managers must not tolerate retaliation

## 12. Investigations

### 12.1 Process
When a violation is reported:
- HR or Legal will investigate promptly
- Employees must cooperate fully
- Confidentiality will be maintained to the extent possible
- Findings will be documented
- Appropriate action will be taken

### 12.2 Disciplinary Action
Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension
- Demotion
- Termination
- Legal action

## 13. Acknowledgment

All employees must:
- Read and understand this Code of Conduct
- Sign an acknowledgment form
- Complete annual training
- Recertify annually
- Ask questions if anything is unclear
