# Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Human Resources Department  
**Version:** 2.1

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for employees who work remotely, either on a regular basis or occasionally. The goal is to ensure productivity, security, and work-life balance while maintaining company standards.

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Full-time Remote Work
Employees may be eligible for full-time remote work if:
- They have been employed for at least 6 months
- Their role does not require physical presence in the office
- They have demonstrated consistent performance and self-management
- Their manager approves the arrangement

### 2.2 Hybrid Work
All employees may work remotely up to 2 days per week with manager approval.

### 2.3 Occasional Remote Work
Occasional remote work (e.g., due to appointments, weather) may be approved on a case-by-case basis.

## 3. Requirements

### 3.1 Workspace
Employees must maintain a dedicated, safe, and ergonomic workspace that:
- Has reliable high-speed internet (minimum 25 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- Is free from distractions during work hours
- Meets basic safety standards
- Has adequate lighting and ventilation

### 3.2 Equipment
- Company will provide a laptop and necessary software
- Employees are responsible for internet connectivity costs
- Additional equipment (monitor, keyboard, mouse) may be provided upon request
- All company equipment must be returned upon termination or transition to in-office work

### 3.3 Availability
Remote employees must:
- Be available during core business hours (10:00 AM - 3:00 PM local time)
- Respond to communications within 2 hours during business hours
- Maintain their calendar with accurate availability
- Attend all required meetings via video conference

## 4. Security Requirements

### 4.1 Data Protection
- All work must be conducted on company-provided devices
- Use of VPN is mandatory when accessing company resources
- Sensitive documents must not be printed at home
- Physical documents must be stored securely and shredded when no longer needed

### 4.2 Confidentiality
- Ensure privacy during video calls and phone conversations
- Do not work in public spaces when handling confidential information
- Use headphones for calls containing sensitive information
- Lock computer when stepping away

### 4.3 Software and Systems
- Keep all software and systems updated
- Use only approved applications and tools
- Report any security incidents immediately
- Complete mandatory security training annually

## 5. Performance and Accountability

### 5.1 Expectations
Remote employees are held to the same performance standards as in-office employees:
- Meet all deadlines and deliverables
- Maintain quality of work
- Participate actively in team activities
- Complete required training and development

### 5.2 Communication
- Check email at least twice daily
- Participate in daily team standups
- Update project management tools regularly
- Be proactive in seeking help and sharing progress

### 5.3 Monitoring
- Managers may conduct regular check-ins
- Performance will be evaluated based on output and results
- Remote work privileges may be revoked for performance issues

## 6. Expenses and Reimbursement

### 6.1 Covered Expenses
The company will reimburse:
- One-time home office setup (up to $500)
- Monthly internet stipend ($50)
- Required software or subscriptions

### 6.2 Non-Covered Expenses
The company will not reimburse:
- Utilities (electricity, heating, cooling)
- Home office furniture (unless pre-approved)
- Personal phone bills
- Coffee or meals

### 6.3 Reimbursement Process
Submit expense reports with receipts within 30 days via the expense management system.

## 7. Time Off and Leave

- Remote employees must follow the same time-off request procedures
- Use the HR system to log all time off
- Notify team members of planned absences
- Set appropriate out-of-office messages

## 8. Termination of Remote Work

The company reserves the right to terminate remote work arrangements:
- With 30 days notice for business needs
- Immediately for policy violations or performance issues
- Upon employee request with 2 weeks notice

## 9. International Remote Work

Employees wishing to work remotely from another country must:
- Obtain approval at least 60 days in advance
- Coordinate with Legal and HR departments
- Understand tax and legal implications
- Limit international remote work to 30 days per year

## 10. Questions and Support

For questions about this policy, contact:
- HR Department: hr@company.com
- IT Support: itsupport@company.com
- Your direct manager

## 11. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and may be updated as needed. Employees will be notified of changes via email and the company intranet.
